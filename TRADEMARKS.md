- refer to PenEcho accurately in articles, tutorials, reviews, and compatibility statements;
- link to the canonical PenEcho repository or website;
- show unaltered screenshots for commentary, education, or reporting; and
- say that another project is "based on PenEcho" or "compatible with PenEcho" when that statement is accurate and does not imply endorsement.

You need prior written permission to:

- use a PenEcho Mark in the name of a product, service, company, domain, package, or social account;

- use a PenEcho Mark on merchandise or in paid advertising as your own brand.

## Forks and redistributed builds

Publicly distributed modified versions must use a distinct product name and visual identity and must not present themselves as an official PenEcho release. They may state factually that they are based on PenEcho. Copyright notices, license notices, and source-code obligations remain governed by the AGPL.

An unmodified redistribution may identify the software as PenEcho, but it must not describe the distributor or build as official unless the project owner has authorized that claim.

For trademark permission, use the contact method published by the owner of the canonical PenEcho repository.
